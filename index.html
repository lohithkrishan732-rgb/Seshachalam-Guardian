Seshachalam Guardian

A game-theoretic simulation of the political economy of conservation

You are Village 1, a forest-fringe VSS cooperative on the edge of the Seshachalam hills. Together with four neighbouring villages you share a single living treasury: 100 Red Sandalwood trees.

A smuggler offers you cash to look the other way. The state offers a ban it cannot enforce. Every round you choose: Guard the forest, or Poach for quick cash. If the forest hits zero, everyone loses.

Can you survive the CITES Export Freeze without breaking the commons?

🏛️ Rounds 1–3: State Ban
🤝 Rounds 4–10: Pre-Funded Escrow
🚨 Rounds 6–8: CITES Stress Test

How the game works

🏛️ Phase 1 — Command & Control (R1–3)

A distant state ban. Guard → +0 cash (you starve). Poach → +20 cash, but 3 trees vanish from the shared forest. Fear of being the last honest villager drives a cascade of defection.

🤝 Phase 2 — Pre-Funded Escrow (R4–10)

International buyers pre-pay into a state escrow. Guard → guaranteed wage from the escrow pool. But if anyone cheats, the DNA ledger flags it and the whole collective's payout is slashed next round. Police your neighbours.

🚨 The CITES Stress Test (R6–8)

An export freeze locks trade. No fresh cash enters escrow — only a draining emergency buffer (2 → 1 → 0 turns left). Meanwhile the smuggler's bribe escalates: 20 → 40 → 60 cash. This is where communities crack.

🏆 Winning

Survive all 10 rounds with the forest above zero. Your score rewards both personal cash and trees left standing. True mastery: get rich and keep the forest alive.
